BIOGRAPHY

1941 - Early Life

Armando Anthony Corea was born on June 12, 1941 in Chelsea, Massachusetts. His father, a jazz trumpeter, introduced him to jazz at an early age. He began studying piano at age 4 and was playing gigs by age 15.

1960s - Breakthrough

After studying at Columbia University and Juilliard, Corea began his professional career playing with Mongo Santamaria and Willie Bobo. He joined Miles Davis' band in 1968, contributing to landmark albums like "In a Silent Way" and "Bitches Brew".

1970s - Fusion Pioneer

Formed the avant-garde group Circle in 1970, then founded Return to Forever in 1972, pioneering jazz fusion. Released classic albums like "Light as a Feather" and "Romantic Warrior" that expanded jazz's boundaries.

1980s - Acoustic Focus

Returned to acoustic jazz with his Elektric Band and Akoustic Band. Collaborated with Gary Burton, Herbie Hancock, and other jazz greats. Won multiple Grammy Awards during this period.

2000s - Legacy

Continued touring and recording prolifically, collaborating with younger musicians. Became one of the most nominated artists in Grammy history with over 60 nominations. Passed away on February 9, 2021 at age 79, leaving behind an immense musical legacy.
