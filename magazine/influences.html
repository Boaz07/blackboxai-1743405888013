INFLUENCES

The musical voices that shaped Chick Corea's distinctive style

Bud Powell

The bebop pioneer was Corea's first major piano influence. Corea studied Powell's recordings intensely, particularly admiring his rhythmic drive and harmonic sophistication.

Thelonious Monk

Monk's unconventional approach to harmony and rhythm deeply impacted Corea's playing. Corea often cited Monk's "unique musical language" as a major inspiration.

Bill Evans

Evans' lyrical approach and impressionistic harmonies were foundational to Corea's ballad playing. Corea called Evans "the most influential pianist after Bud Powell."

Classical Music

Corea studied Mozart, Beethoven, and Bartók extensively. Their compositional techniques influenced his approach to form and development in jazz improvisation.

Latin Music

Corea's Cuban heritage and early exposure to Latin jazz artists like Tito Puente and Machito shaped his rhythmic sensibility and love for Afro-Cuban grooves.

Miles Davis

Playing in Davis' band transformed Corea's approach to electronic instruments and modal playing. Davis' concept of "space" in music became central to Corea's philosophy.

"All the music I've heard has influenced me in some way. From Mozart to The Beatles to John Coltrane."

— Chick Corea
