TECHNIQUES

The innovative approaches that defined Chick Corea's piano mastery

Harmonic Language

Spanish-tinged Harmony

Corea frequently incorporated flamenco-inspired harmonies using Phrygian mode and Andalusian cadences (i-♭VII-♭VI-V), creating his signature "Spanish" sound.

Fourth-based Voicings

He developed a distinctive approach to chord voicings built in fourths rather than traditional thirds, creating an open, modern sound.

Rhythmic Innovation

Metric Modulation

Corea was a master of shifting between time signatures and tempos seamlessly, often using rhythmic displacement to create tension.

Latin Jazz Fusion

He blended Afro-Cuban rhythms with jazz harmony, particularly favoring the Mozambique rhythm and complex clave patterns.

Improvisational Concepts

Motivic Development

Corea's solos often built from small melodic cells that he would develop, transpose, and reharmonize throughout his improvisations.

Space and Silence

Influenced by Miles Davis, he mastered the art of leaving space in his solos, creating dramatic tension and release.

Keyboard Setup

Yamaha CP-70

Corea was instrumental in popularizing this electric grand piano, using its unique tone for both acoustic and electric contexts.

Minimoog

His pioneering use of synthesizers in jazz fusion expanded the vocabulary of keyboardists in the 1970s.

Signature Lick

Corea's trademark lick combining chromatic approach notes with fourth voicings:

RH: E♭-D-D♭-C | B♭-E♭-A♭-D♭

LH: Rootless voicing in fourths
